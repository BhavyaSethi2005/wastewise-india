ITEM: Agarbatti / incense sticks (used, burnt)
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Agarbatti is made from bamboo stick + natural binding paste + fragrance material — all biodegradable and compostable. The ash is also organic.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Dry bin because it is a stick and feels dry.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. The ash and stick will compost. Also usable as garden fertilizer.
PREPARATION: Ensure fully extinguished and cooled.

ITEM: Dhoop / sambrani (incense cone ash and residue)
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Made from natural resins, herbs, and organic binding agents. Fully biodegradable.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Reject bin or throwing on ground.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. Ash can also be used for garden composting.
PREPARATION: Cool fully before disposal.

ITEM: Clay diya (after Diwali / puja — used)
CATEGORY: Inert / Construction waste or Green bin
BIN: Green bin (if unglazed terracotta only)
WHY: Unglazed terracotta is natural clay — biodegradable back into soil. Glazed or painted diyas with synthetic paint are inert waste.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Mixed bin or left on road indefinitely.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Unglazed plain diyas → bury in garden or green bin (returns to soil). Painted/glazed → dry/inert bin.
PREPARATION: Check if glazed or painted. Remove any remaining oil/wax first.

ITEM: Puja flowers and garlands (used)
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Natural flowers and leaves are fully compostable. However, synthetic decorations mixed in are NOT.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Immersing in rivers — causes algal blooms and oxygen depletion.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. Never immerse in water bodies. Remove any plastic/synthetic elements.
PREPARATION: Separate real flowers from plastic decorations, synthetic string, foil wrappers.

ITEM: Coconut (offered in puja — whole, broken)
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Coconut husk, shell, and water are all fully biodegradable and compostable.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Dumping at temple premises or on roadsides.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. Coconut shell is excellent for home composting or as a fuel in rural areas.
PREPARATION: Break into pieces for faster composting.

ITEM: Paan wrapper and paan masala pouch (gutka packet)
CATEGORY: Reject / Non-recyclable
BIN: Dry bin (reject — non-recyclable)
WHY: Metallised plastic film + tobacco/chemical residue. Same as chip packet — impossible to recycle. Tobacco residue makes it hazardous.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Spitting on roads and throwing pouch anywhere — major urban litter problem.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Dry/reject bin. These are extremely difficult to recycle anywhere in the world.
PREPARATION: None.

ITEM: Supari (betel nut) shells and waste
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Natural plant matter — fully biodegradable.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Mixing with gutka packets (which are not compostable).
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. Compostable.
PREPARATION: Separate from any packaging.

ITEM: Old steel tiffin box / dabba (dented, unusable)
CATEGORY: Dry / Recyclable Waste (Scrap Metal)
BIN: Blue bin or directly to kabadiwala
WHY: Stainless steel is 100% recyclable indefinitely. High value scrap metal.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Keeping unused in kitchen forever.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Blue bin or directly to kabadiwala for better value. Clean first.
PREPARATION: Clean thoroughly. Remove rubber gasket if present (dry bin separately).

ITEM: Brass puja items (broken, old lamps, idols)
CATEGORY: Dry / Recyclable (Scrap Metal)
BIN: Blue bin or directly to scrap dealer
WHY: Brass (copper + zinc alloy) is 100% recyclable. High scrap value.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Discarding as sentiment — these are valuable recyclable metals.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: If usable → donate to temple or NGO. If not → kabadiwala or scrap dealer.
PREPARATION: Clean of any puja materials.

ITEM: Dried rangoli / kolam powder (leftover)
CATEGORY: Organic or Inert depending on type
BIN: Green bin (natural) / Dry bin (synthetic)
WHY: Natural rangoli made from rice flour, chalk, or flower petals is compostable. Synthetic chemical-dyed rangoli is inert/reject.
COMMON_MISTAKE: All rangoli thrown together.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Natural (rice/chalk/flower based) → green bin. Synthetic dyed powders → dry/reject bin.
PREPARATION: Identify material type — check packaging.

ITEM: Mud / clay from festival idols (Ganesh, Durga — immersion waste)
CATEGORY: Organic / Inert
BIN: Garden soil or green bin
WHY: Natural clay idol = returns to earth naturally. BUT: painted idols with chemical paints, POP (Plaster of Paris) idols are HIGHLY TOXIC to water bodies.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Immersing POP or painted idols in rivers — destroys aquatic ecosystems (major problem in India).
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Natural clay → garden or green bin. POP/synthetic → NEVER immerse in water. Check municipal artificial pond programs.
PREPARATION: Identify natural clay vs POP. Natural clay = off-white, soft. POP = very white, hard.

ITEM: Old mattress (foam or cotton)
CATEGORY: Bulk Waste — special collection
BIN: NOT any standard bin — municipal bulk collection
WHY: Too large for standard bins. Cotton fill can be composted. Foam is synthetic and non-recyclable. Springs are scrap metal.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Dumping on roadside — extremely common and creates illegal dumping grounds.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Contact municipal bulk item collection. Cotton fill → kabadiwala (has value). Old mattress dealers sometimes buy for refilling.
PREPARATION: Call municipality for bulk pickup schedule.

ITEM: Holi colours / gulal (dry powder)
CATEGORY: Depends on type — Organic or Chemical Hazardous
BIN: Green bin (natural) / Red bin (synthetic)
WHY: Traditional natural gulal made from flowers is compostable. Modern synthetic colors often contain mica, heavy metals (lead, mercury), industrial dyes — hazardous.
COMMON_MISTAKE: All gulal treated the same.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Natural flower/herbal gulal → green bin. Synthetic (bright/neon colors) → red bin.
PREPARATION: Check packaging — natural brands specify "herbal" or "organic."

ITEM: Old newspaper used as base in pooja thali
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste (if soiled with ghee, haldi, kumkum)
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Once soiled with puja materials (turmeric, kumkum, ghee), the paper is contaminated and cannot be recycled.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Blue bin because it is newspaper.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin — the organic materials will compost along with the paper.
PREPARATION: None.

ITEM: Aluminium foil used in puja / bhandara (food service)
CATEGORY: Dry / Recyclable IF clean, Reject if food-soiled
BIN: Blue bin (clean) / Green bin (food-soiled)
WHY: Aluminium is fully recyclable. But food-soiled foil contaminates recycling batches.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Blue bin without checking cleanliness.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Lightly used/clean → scrunch test (if it stays scrunched, recycle) → blue bin. Food-soiled → green bin.
PREPARATION: The scrunch test: scrunch into a ball. If it springs back = too thin to recycle. If it stays scrunched = recyclable.

ITEM: Coconut shell (dried, after use)
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste OR biomass fuel
BIN: Green bin
WHY: 100% biodegradable. Also has value as activated carbon source.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Dry bin or burning openly.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. Better: home compost (slow but excellent carbon source). Or sell to coconut shell charcoal makers.
PREPARATION: None.

ITEM: Used filter coffee decoction grounds
CATEGORY: Organic / Wet Waste
BIN: Green bin
WHY: Coffee grounds are rich in nitrogen — excellent compost material. Used heavily as garden fertilizer.
COMMON_MISTAKE: None usually — but don't flush down sink (clogs pipes over time).
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Green bin. Or directly in garden soil. Excellent slug repellent too.
PREPARATION: Let cool slightly. Do not flush.

ITEM: Old saree / dhoti / kurta (torn, unwearable, natural fiber)
CATEGORY: Textile Waste — organic if pure cotton
BIN: NOT regular bins
WHY: Pure cotton/silk is fully compostable. Synthetic (polyester, nylon) is not.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Dry bin. Or burning.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Wearable → Goonj or local donations. Pure cotton/silk torn → cut into cleaning rags (kitchen use, cleaning) or compost. Synthetic blends → textile recycling banks.
PREPARATION: Check label — cotton/silk vs synthetic. Cut into rags if pure cotton.

ITEM: Thermocol (thermocol box from sweets / mithai shops)
CATEGORY: Reject / Dry waste — non-recyclable locally
BIN: Dry bin (reject — not recyclable in most Indian cities)
WHY: EPS (expanded polystyrene) is technically recyclable but no collection infrastructure exists in most Indian cities. Very high volume, very low weight = uneconomical to collect.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Blue bin thinking it will be recycled.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Reuse for cold storage, packing fragile items. When unusable → dry/reject bin.
PREPARATION: Reuse first. Never burn — releases toxic styrene gas.

ITEM: Bidi and cigarette butts
CATEGORY: Hazardous / Reject
BIN: Dry bin (not in organic — toxic)
WHY: Cigarette filters are non-biodegradable plastic (cellulose acetate) filled with toxic tar, nicotine, and heavy metals. One butt can contaminate 40 litres of water. Bidi has natural leaf but chemical residue.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Ground disposal — most common litter item in India.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: Dry/reject bin. TerraCycle has cigarette butt recycling in some cities. Never in organic/wet bin.
PREPARATION: Ensure completely extinguished first.

ITEM: Old mobile phone SIM cards
CATEGORY: E-Waste
BIN: E-waste collection
WHY: Contains gold, silver, and trace electronics. Small but toxic if landfilled.
COMMON_MISTAKE: Throwing in any bin or leaving unused in drawers.
CORRECT_DISPOSAL: E-waste collection. Or: destroy physically (scissors) before disposing to protect data.
PREPARATION: Ensure no data remains. Cut through chip area before disposal.
